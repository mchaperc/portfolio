Pieces of Note:

NOTE: Each of the following pieces have recordings that you may listen to along with the written descriptions. In order to play the recordings, press the number that corresponds with each to begin playback ("1" for "The Right", "2" for "Among the Stars," and "3" for "To Build Anew." If you wish to stop playback, press the corresponding number again.

1) The Right

"The Right" was written for the 2014 Union County High School Indoor Percussion Ensemble's competitive show. With it, they won the 2014 State Championship, CIPA Championship (schools from SC, NC, TN, and VA), and finished 14th place at the WGI World Championships. The show utilizes the word "Right" and attempts to play off of it many different ways: right hand only passages, visual representations of arrows pointing to the right, the "Rite of Spring," Civil Rights, Miranda Rights, and Individual Rights guaranteed in the Constitution. Listen for patriotic tunes strewn about to emphasize the individual rights, Igor Stravinsky's "Rite of Spring," and an African Spiritual over which vocal samples from Martin Luther King Jr's "I have a Dream" speech hammer home the message.

2) Among the Stars

A motto I learned from a mentor of mine years ago reads something like this: "If you shoot for the moon and miss, you'll land among the stars." My interpretation is a principle by which I've tried to live my life and one I've taught my students. This was a show that was written for the 2014 UCHS Marching Band's competitive show. "Among the Stars" is the story of a child who dreams of someday becoming an astronaut. The show starts with a child staring off into space. Children's themes are played in the background as the child drifts to sleep. From there, a launch sequence is initiated and the dream begins. Follow along as the child visits places like the moon and two planets (Jupiter and Mars - where the child will encounter aliens!), courtesy of Gustav Holst's suite "The Planets." Vocal samples, such as: "Someday when I grow up, I want to be an astronaut," or "Houston, we have a problem," are scattered throughout and serve as narration for key moments.

3) The Teller's Story:

This piece was written for the inaugural season of the Limestone College indoor percussion ensemble, Saints Percussion. "The Teller's Story" is a quick look into the life of poet Edgar Allen Poe. It takes the audience on a tour of the early years of his life: from his enrollment into university where he fell in love with writing, to meeting his wife and once again falling in love, to her tragic death and his descent into madness.

Portfolio

Pre Iron Yard

Here is a small sampling of projects I completed prior to enrolling in The Iron Yard.

  • Etch-a-Sketch:

    An exercise in basic JavaScript and jQuery, it replicates the childhood toy that could be used to create very pixelated drawings. Utilizes HTML5, JavaScript, and jQuery.

  • Shopping List:

    Much as it sounds, this project was intended to be a simple app for storing a grocery list, as well as allowing the user to generate a rough estimate for the cost of a shopping trip. Utilizes HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, JavaScript, and jQuery.

  • FizzBuzz:

    Just a little creation to display basic JavaScript programming knowledge. The point of the "game" is to enter a maximum number, then the program will print Fizz for numbers divisible by 3, Buzz for numbers divisible by 5, FizzBuzz for numbers divisible by both, and the number if it isn't divisible by either. Utilizes HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and jQuery.

  • Hot or Cold Game:

    This game asks for an input between 1 and 100. It will then randomly generate a number and check the accuracy of your guess. It will provide feedback based on your proximity to the generated number while keeping track of already guessed numbers and guess attempts. Utilizes HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, JavaScript, and jQuery.

  • JavaScript Quiz:

    This time around, the quiz app in interactive - it keeps track of progress, score, and loads one question at a time. Each question is stored in and retrieved from JS objects. Utilizes HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and jQuery.

  • Waitstaff Calculator:

    This is my first "real" project in Angular. It utilizes a module and controller approach, removing the logic from the markup. Utilizes HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and Angular.

After Enrollment

This is a sampling of projects I've worked on since enrolling at The Iron Yard.

  • Pixel Perfect:

    This project required that I accurately replicate, to the pixel, a design that was provided. This required to the use of color grabbers, pixel measurments, etc... The end result is NOT responsive, as it was designed for a specific screen measurement. Utilizes HTML5 and CSS3.

  • Responsive Design:

    My first real foray into responsive design, this site was a lesson in gridded layouts and media queries while also learning our first CSS preprocessor. Utilizes HTML5, SCSS, Bourbon, Neat, JavaScript.

  • Responsive Calculator:

    This assignment asked that we replicate the functionality of a calculator, but that it needed to also be responsive to screen size. Utilizes HTML5, SCSS, Bourbon, Neat, JavaScript.

  • jQuery Keyboard:

    An attempt at recreating the functionality of a QWERTY keyboard through the use of jQuery keypress and click events. Utilizes HTML5, SCSS, Bourbon, Neat, JavaScript, and jQuery.

  • CMS Blog:

    An important function of many front-end apps is CRUD functionality. This app is a custom Content Management System Blog allowing for the user to Create, Read, Update, and Delete posts. This data is persisted to a server. Utilizes HTML5, SCSS, Bourbon, Neat, JavaScript, jQuery, Underscore, and Backbone.

  • Todo App:

    Another CRUD app, this one expands upon the previous apps functionality by integrating into it child views. This allows for each item to be manipulated apart from the others This data is persisted to a server.. Utilizes HTML5, SCSS, Bourbon, Neat, JavaScript, jQuery, Underscore, and Backbone.

  • Restaurant Menu:

    The objective here was to create a restaurant app that allowed the user to view a menu, whose contents are received from a BAAS (Parse), add items to a virtual shopping cart, and submit orders. These orders are persisted to the server, which can be viewd in the admin portal accessible from the main page. Users can also create an account via the user page. Utilizes HTML5, SCSS, Bourbon, Neat, JavaScript, jQuery, Underscore, Backbone, and Parse.
